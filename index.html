About

Hi there and welcome from Sydney. My name is Vicki Zhong and this website is a bit of a summer project of mine.

I am currently an engineering student at the University of New South Wales. But apart from getting my head around all my lecture material, I also love to go for walks and to visit places around Sydney (esp. cafes because who doesn't love food). Check out some of my photos of the state library, Sydney Harbour from Mrs Macquarie's Chair, food, and heaps more snaps I have taken here and there.

I love to learn about Asian cultures. I am a true believer that we should never stop learning.

I just have this natural draw to the little slices of information I can learn about the past and take special interest in Chinese history. I love to try Asian food, watch Chinese television series, read classical texts and understand important figures. At the end of 2016, I had the opportunity to spend three months at Tsinghua University in Beijing as part of a research exchange program and finally got to visit the Forbidden City and Great Wall and experience so much. I also took a little trip to Xi'an, the ancient capital city and enjoyed that a lot as well.

I love computers, especially information systems and databases. I've been working on my web development skills but have to admit that when it comes to the digital arena, I am absolutely hopeless with games.

Professional

Education

I am a student at the University of New South Wales (UNSW) undertaking a Bachelor of Engineering (Materials Engineering )/ Masters of Biomedical Engineering combined degree.

I enjoyed small success whilst at Sydney Girls High School, where I graduated with an ATAR of 99.65. I am proud to call myself a Sydney High 'Old Girl' and have so many beautiful memories of the six years there. I then decided that I was more suited to the technically-oriented career paths so I endeavoured on my journey through an engineering degree. With a small scholarship, some online searching and heavy convincing from a PhD student, I found myself in a Materialx Engineering prgram. I have always had a passion for the health and disease area so I picked a combined degree that allowed me to be enrolled in biomedical engineering as well.

So far I have had courses spanning areas of Engineering Mechanics, Microscopy and Materials Characterisation, Thermodynamics, Fluid Flow and Heat Transfer, Phase Equilibria, Polymers, Ceramic Processing, just to name a few. On the biomedical engineering side, I have taken courses on Engineering in Medicine, Clinical Laboratory Science, Clinical Information Systems, Physiology and will go into biomechanics, imaging and more in the upcoming year.
This paragraph probably doesn't make much sense to people outside of the degree but the bottom line is
I am learning lots and enjoying it.

I am going into my thesis year in 2018. My project will be on computational modelling of Cochlear implants — I am quite excited to be working on biomedical technologies. I also intend on doing a Masters project in biomedical engineering the year after.
